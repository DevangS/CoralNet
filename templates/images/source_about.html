Sources

A Source is CoralNet's name for a coral analysis project. Generally, a Source will contain all of a research group's coral images from a particular world location. Images are organized by their specific location and date. That way, you can compare annotation statistics from the same location between different years.

To get started, create a Source or get invited to a Source. You can choose to make your Source public or private. Once you have a Source, you'll create a labelset, which is the set of labels that you'll use to annotate your images. Image annotation means identifying points of the image: as Acropora coral, Porites coral, algae, and so on.

Once you have a Source and labelset, you can upload images and start annotating them, using our in-browser annotation tool. You can also upload both images and annotations if you have already annotated your images. Then, you can view annotation statistics on your Source or a subset of images: how much Porites there was in 2009, the trend of coral numbers from 2008 to 2009, and so on.
