Basics

The text fields on the right show the current labels for each point.
If the text in the field is in black, the point is human-annotated. Once all points are human-annotated, annotation for the image is finished.
If the text is in gray, the point is robot-annotated and needs to be confirmed by a human annotator.
If the field background is in red, the text is not a valid label code and needs to be changed.

The image shows the locations of the points. By default, different colors distinguish between unannotated and robot-annotated points, human-annotated points, and selected points. You can change the color settings to display robot-annotated points in a different color, as well.

Choosing a point to label
  • Click in one of the text fields on the right.
  • Move to the next or previous point's text field with the ↓ and ↑ arrow keys.
  • You can also Shift-click on the image to select a point.
Labeling the chosen point
  • Below the image is a grid of label buttons - there's one button for each label in your Source's labelset. Click the button corresponding to the label you want to use.
  • You may also type the label code directly into the text field.
  • You may hold Ctrl and use the arrow keys ← → ↑ ↓ to choose a label by navigating the button grid.
Confirming or changing a label
  • Press EnterReturn to confirm a robot-annotated label and move onto the next point.
Saving your work
  • To save your work, click the "Save Progress" button on the lower right, or hit Ctrl+S Cmd+S. There are two cases where you won't be able to save:
    • When no changes have been made. In this case, the button text will be "Saved".
    • When any point is labeled with an invalid label code. In this case, the button text will be "Error".
Selecting and labeling multiple points
  • Select multiple points by Ctrl-clicking Cmd-clicking the points on the image.
  • You may also click the numbers next to the text fields.
  • For more multi-select options, use these buttons on the upper right: , , and .
  • Once multiple points are selected, label them all by clicking a label button.
Getting a better look at a point
  • To zoom in, left-click on the image or press Shift+↑.
  • To zoom out, right-clickCtrl-click on the image or press Shift+↓.
  • You can also zoom with these buttons on the upper right: , , and .
  • To toggle between different point display modes, use these buttons on the upper right: , , and .
  • There are two ways to adjust the Brightness and Contrast values: use the sliders, or type a value in the text field. Click Apply to apply the new values. Click Reset Parameters to reset both to 0.
Navigate to another image
  • The bottom-right part of the annotation tool has links to navigate between images.
  • To navigate to the next image needing annotation, click "Go to next image", or press g and then n.
  • The annotation tool tracks your navigation history as you go between images, similar to an internet browser.
  • To navigate back in this history, click "Back", or press g and then b.
  • To navigate forward, click "Forward", or press g and then f.
Settings
  • To open the annotation tool settings, click the Settings button (next to the Help/Controls button).
